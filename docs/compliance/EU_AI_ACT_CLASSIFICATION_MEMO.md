# EU AI Act — internal classification memo (pre-launch)

**Document type:** Internal compliance record (not legal advice)  
**Subject:** Proposed non–high-risk classification of the TrailSafe / outdoor risk–information product  
**Status:** Draft for retention before public launch; revise if product scope, embedding, or decision-making role changes.

---

## 1. Problem statement

Regulators or courts could argue that the application should be classified as **high-risk** under the EU Artificial Intelligence Act, because it can influence user choices in contexts where **physical injury or death** is a foreseeable (though not intended) background risk. This memorandum records internal reasons **not** to treat the system as high-risk in the current design, to support defensible documentation if questioned.

## 2. Recorded arguments (verbatim)

The following three points are kept **verbatim** as the formal on-record reasoning:

1. "This system does not fall within any category listed in Annex III of the EU AI Act. Outdoor recreational risk prediction is not enumerated among the eight listed high-risk use cases."

2. "This system is not embedded as a safety component in a product regulated under Annex I of the EU AI Act (e.g., medical devices, machinery, vehicles). It is a standalone consumer mobile application."

3. "This system does not materially influence the outcome of decision-making within the meaning of Article 6(3). The final decision to undertake any outdoor activity rests entirely with the user. The app provides informational inputs; it does not determine, authorize, or prohibit any course of action."
